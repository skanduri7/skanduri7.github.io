This project explores the relationship between perspective, focal length, zoom, and camera position through three shoots and a dolly zoom effect.

Overview

Below, I present photographs demonstrating how camera distance and zoom affect perspective. I also created a short animated GIF that reproduces the dolly zoom. Each section includes my photos and reflections.

Part 1: Wrong way vs. Right way

Setup I started by taking a close up picture. Then I stepped back several feet and zoomed in and then framed to a similar face size. The second image should show more natural facial proportions.

Observations

Moving back and zooming in changes perspective lines and relative proportions even when the face occupies similar pixels. The longer focal length reduces exaggeration of near features compared to far features .

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

Setup I first zoomed in from far away down a long driveway in front of a house. Then I walked forward and shot again without zoom so that the scene filled a similar portion of the frame. The telephoto image should feel flatter; the wide image accentuates depth.

Observations

The compression is a perspective effect from camera position.

Part 3: Dolly Zoom

Setup I captured a couple still images while moving the camera backward and zooming in so the subject stayed a similar size. I then combined them into an animated GIF.
